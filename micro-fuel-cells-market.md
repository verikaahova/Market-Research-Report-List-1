<p><p>The micro fuel cells market is primarily divided into two types: air-cooled and liquid-cooled micro fuel cells. Air-cooled micro fuel cells utilize ambient air for cooling, making them lightweight and suitable for portable applications. They typically offer simplicity and lower maintenance. In contrast, liquid-cooled micro fuel cells use a liquid medium for cooling, providing better thermal management and efficiency, which is ideal for high-performance applications. Both types cater to diverse industries, including portable electronics, automotive, and backup power systems.</p></p>

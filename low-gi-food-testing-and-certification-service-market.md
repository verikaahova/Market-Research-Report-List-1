<p><p>Low GI food testing and certification services assess foods based on their glycemic index (GI), glycemic load (GL), and insulin index (II) values. The GI value test measures how quickly carbohydrates in food raise blood sugar levels, helping identify low-GI foods suitable for maintaining stable energy. The GL value test accounts for both the quality and quantity of carbohydrates, providing a more holistic view of food impact. The II value test evaluates foods based on their effect on insulin response, crucial for managing metabolic health.</p></p>

<p><p>Head protection equipment is categorized into three main types based on electrical resistance and impact protection: Class A, Class B, and Class C helmets. Class A helmets provide protection from both impact and electrical hazards, suitable for general construction use. Class B helmets offer similar impact protection but with greater electrical insulation, ideal for utility work. Class C helmets focus primarily on impact resistance without electrical insulation, often used in non-hazardous environments like manufacturing. Each type is tailored to specific workplace safety needs.</p></p>

<p><strong>The Mechanical CPR Devices Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Electric</li><li>Pneumatic</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Mechanical CPR devices are classified into two main types: electric and pneumatic. Electric devices use battery-powered motors to deliver consistent chest compressions, ensuring optimal depth and rate, enhancing the efficiency of CPR during emergencies. Pneumatic devices rely on compressed air to function, offering a powerful and effective alternative for performing high-quality chest compressions. Both types are crucial for improving patient outcomes in cardiac arrest situations by providing continuous, hands-free CPR, allowing healthcare providers to focus on other critical interventions.</p></p>

<p><p>The inorganic conductive adhesive market consists of three primary types based on curing reaction. Curing reaction type adhesives typically rely on chemical processes that harden under specific conditions, offering strong conductivity post-curing. Hot melt adhesives, on the other hand, provide a thermoplastic solution that can be re-melted, making them versatile for various applications. High temperature sintering adhesives involve a process that fuses materials at elevated temperatures, ideal for demanding environments that require exceptional thermal and electrical stability.</p></p>

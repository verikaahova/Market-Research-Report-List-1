<p><p>The skin and mucous membrane disinfectants market is categorized into two main types: alcohol-based and non-alcohol types. Alcohol-based disinfectants primarily utilize ethanol or isopropyl alcohol to effectively kill pathogens, offering rapid action and quick evaporation. They are commonly used in healthcare settings for skin antisepsis. In contrast, non-alcohol disinfectants contain alternative active ingredients, such as chlorine compounds or quaternary ammonium compounds. These options provide effective disinfection with less irritation, making them suitable for sensitive skin or specific applications where alcohol is contraindicated.</p></p>
